I am a Sr. Principal at [AWS](https://aws.amazon.com/) and [Gold Jacket holder](https://www.credly.com/users/junjie-tang.0c4593de/badges), working with the largest automotive and technology companies to build production AI systems at scale.

I created the [Autonomous Driving Data Framework (ADDF)](https://github.com/aws-samples/autonomous-driving-data-framework), an open-source platform used by OEMs including BMW Group and Qualcomm to process petabytes of autonomous driving data on AWS. My recent research, [Kernel Forge](/blog/2026/beating-claude-opus-kernel-generation-nki-agent/), uses reinforcement learning to generate hardware-optimized kernels for AWS Trainium that outperform Claude Opus 4.5.

I have spoken at [AWS re:Invent](https://reinvent.awsevents.com/) every year since 2017, at the United Nations on AI for Road Safety, and have published 20+ technical articles on AWS architecture, autonomous driving, and generative AI. I hold [all active AWS certifications](https://www.credly.com/users/junjie-tang.0c4593de/badges) and received the AWS Technical Field Community Collaboration Award (2021).

I regularly share my work on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/junjie-tang/), [Medium](https://medium.com/@junjie-tang), and in [blog](/blog/) posts covering topics from LLM inference and autonomous driving to cloud data architectures.

Previously, I studied at the University of Cologne. I am fluent in English, German, and Chinese.
